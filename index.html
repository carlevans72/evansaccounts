Our Philosophy

Why we do
what we do.

We set up Evans Accounts with a simple goal: to provide professional, reliable and affordable tax and accounting services to owner-managed businesses.

We understand that running a business today means dealing with increasing regulations and administrative tasks. These responsibilities can take up valuable time and energy that is better spent running your business.

That's where we come in. We work closely with you to offer tailored support and trusted advice — helping to reduce the burden of compliance so you can focus on what matters most: growing and running your business.

As a qualified professional, Sharon is bound by strict regulatory standards. This includes maintaining complete client confidentiality and staying fully up to date with the latest legislation and best practices — so you can feel confident you're receiving expert, compliant advice.

What we offer

Our Services

Bookkeeping, VAT & Making Tax Digital

We support day-to-day record keeping and quarterly VAT submissions. Whether you need a fully managed bookkeeping service or just help submitting your figures from your own software, we'll adapt to your needs.

Accounts

We prepare statutory year-end accounts for limited companies and handle online submission to Companies House. We also prepare annual accounts for sole traders to support their personal tax returns.

Taxation

We take care of corporation tax and personal tax returns, including all supporting calculations. We can also assist with capital gains tax reporting to ensure everything is accurate and submitted on time.

Payroll & CIS

Our payroll services include monthly processing, auto-enrolment and year-end reporting. We also provide support with Construction Industry Scheme (CIS) returns and payments.

About Us

Our Director, Sharon Evans, brings over 35 years of experience in accounting and taxation. She is both a Chartered Accountant and a Chartered Tax Advisor, with a career spanning small, medium and large accountancy practices across Scotland.
